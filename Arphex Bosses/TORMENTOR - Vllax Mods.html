The TORMENTOR is a SUPERBOSS entity - the most powerful endgame boss of the mod. Original full name is the "Transcendental Tormentor", but the final name simplifies this.

its design is primarily a mixture of centipede, sea spider, scorpion, and crane fly elements, optimised for an eldritch horror appearance. It has no face or jaws, and due to being a godlike entity it does not need to walk or interact with terrain (floats through it instead). The leg-like appendages are more like tendrils.

Click here for a guide on how to defeat this boss. Summary: Use maximum ranged firepower and mobility items, and circle behind it for respite to heal.

Overview

"SCARY STRONG". The TORMENTOR is an ultimate enormous boss with an extreme level of strength. Its strength is such that the fight can be viewed as more akin to fending off an infection mod than a single boss - it even summons (stronger versions of!) some of the other bosses as minions. Visually, its scale is vast, stretched across an area comparable to that of a biome, with an even more enormous cloud of dark particles around it that spread out over further distances (this cloud is visible whenever the tormentor is active and has a primary target, even if the entity itself is not spawned). When you get close to the Tormentor, the sky increasingly darkens and red stars appear around you, while the sun morphs into a red eclipse texture.

SPAWNING AND TIERS

An "initial" version can rarely spawn in the overworld after you kill the first three bosses (Spider Moth, Scorpioid Bloodluster, and Draconic Voidlasher) - this version can trigger the full Tormentor "tier 1" invasion if looked at directly. Or alternatively, the Tormentor can be summoned from a quartz altar, found rarely on the bottom layer of The Crawling Dimension, by throwing an Abyssal Crystal down the hole into the void for tier 1 (recommended first time), or a Fire Opal for tier 2 (five tiers are planned in total, so there will be tiers for the other minerals in future. Tier 2 has much more dangerous attacks than tier 1, but comes with double the rewards - two tormentor levels and two core drops. When summoned via the altar, the Tormentor will automatically set its primary following target to the nearest player to the altar.

ELIMINATING

The Tormentor can be killed or sealed (sealing is much easier!). To seal it, the primary tormentor target must travel to an overworld Crawling Portal and a Bane of the Darkness must be deposited into the portal. If in multiplayer and the primary target refuses to do so, you can kill them and it may switch target. To kill it, you need to use some of the most powerful weapons from the mod, like the Genesis Rifle, Chrono Cannon, and Singularity Scythe, and boss-level armour will be critical - Spacetime Armour or above is recommended for flight ability and resistance. These three weapons are effective, and the first two can work even better if dual-wielded. Its comparatively small hitbox inside its giant black maw is indicated by a red glowing sphere and tends to move up and down. Each time you kill the Tormentor, you gain a fraction of its power for use in any post-tormentor weapons like the Tormentor Summoner and Tormented Wrath.

Beware: It is invincible in the Crawling Dimension! Its avatars must be fought elsewhere to be killable!

MECHANICS

Game mechanics for this entity are complex. It latches on to a single survival/adventure mode player as its primary target, then follows them across unlimited distances and between dimensions (it should be able to travel to any dimension). This creates a sense of it being an omnipresent threat to its target that cannot be escaped without sealing or killing it. The primary target of the Tormentor will emit small moving black particles, making them identifiable. Attacking the Tormentor comes with a 1 in 20 chance per hit to make a player the new primary target.

Any players near to it (especially its target) will be subjects of "torment", getting a potion effect that gives occasional damage and debuffs. Its primary target will receive the worst of the torment, alternating between raining spiders on their head, summoning swarms of locusts around them, spawning amped versions of various arthropods nearby, and a cycle where it flashes repeatedly on the screen and strikes lightning down upon them. However, all of this only occurs if they are out in the open, and the intensity depends on what bosses the player has killed (if you've killed the Spider Moth, you're eligible for spider rain, if you've killed the Scorpioid, you're eligible for lightning strikes, and if you've killed the Voidlasher, you're eligible for boosted enemies being spawned nearby (note that if the Tormentor was marked as player-spawned, these limits are bypassed!). If the primary target player is underneath a block or does not have at least 10 armour points, the tormenting cycles will not occur for them if in the overworld. When you are at risk of being "tormented", a black vignette becomes increasingly prominent.

If you attack the Tormentor (its comparatively small hitbox core is marked by a red sphere), it will enter its battle mode, using numerous kinds of attacks (see abilities below) to destroy any opposition. Some of its attacks can lock onto multiple targets at once, since it does not use a conventional means of setting attack targets. If a player is a tormentor attack target, they will lose this status after 15 minutes, and it will go back to tormenting instead of attacking. Or if a targeted player dies, they will get a full minute of "respite" before the tormentor and its summons start attacking them again. In tier 2, it can split its red core into two, and they spin around the entity, making it harder to attack as these are moving targets.

On death, it drops an ultimate item, the Core of Eternal Suffering, a huge amount of XP, and registers a Tormentor kill for each Tormentor attack-targeted player in the same dimension (plus notifies in chat and emits golden particles around registered players). You can accumulate "tormentor kills", and the Core item shows how many you have when held. The more Tormentor levels, the stronger your post-Tormentor tools will become.

Summonable with commands, marking itself as spawned by player if spawned in close proximity (this means it will go straight to full health as opposed to setting its initial health to that of the global tormentor health variable).

Abilities

The abilities of the TORMENTOR include:

  • Ability to follow its target between dimensions and unlimited distances. It is not constrained to any single location, acting more as an omnipresent deity.
  • Infinite render distance and fog turns dark red when nearby (increases fog distance when far away) - You can see it from 30,000+ blocks away! Some other mods may interfere with these features, especially mods that modify fog rendering.
  • Major damage resistance. Cannot take more than ten damage per hit unless the hit is a billion damage or more (enabling /kill, which deactivates and despawns the Tormentor), or if a high level Tormented Wrath is used (up to 50 damage). Stifles damage massively, forcing a long, challenging fight. 1 second of damage resistance after each time it takes a hit (configurable and can still take damage from minions being killed during this period).
  • Monstrous melee attack damage. If you get too close to the maw hitbox, it will deal 10,000 attack damage or a third of its attack target's health per hit, whichever is larger. The damage is armour piercing.
  • Lifesteal - Steals health whenever one of its attacks lands on an entity, recovering a health point (loses this ability when below 50hp).
  • Total damage immunity from behind. Can only be damaged from in front in the red glowing sphere hitbox in the maw.
  • Some of its attacks give a temporary screen blurring shader to targets on impact.
  • Various devastating attacks shoot from its huge maw - it shuffles across attack modes in a random order each time. Note that some of these attacks can only fire forward out of the maw, and it will not fire them backwards. However, given that it is invincible from behind, this benefit won't provide an option to easily defeat the boss (but perfect opportunity to recover health by moving out of its field of view). These attacks include:
    1. Heavy Torment Blasts - Default mode attack. These enormous explosive blasts resemble the Core of Eternal Suffering, dealing extreme damage and exploding on contact. For tier 2, three of these blasts are fired at once, and they deal more damage.
    2. Light Torment Meteor Blasts - Default mode attack. These smaller explosive blasts deal significant damage and rain down towards targets like a meteor shower, occasionally exploding midair to deal damage without direct contact, like anti-aircraft weaponry. Black with tiny white lines around them). For tier 2, these become more frequent.
    3. Monstrous Torment Sphere - Splay mode attack when below half health. The ultimate ranged attack of the Tormentor - it fires a gargantuan sun-like sphere at its nearest target that grows larger and larger, melting every entity within its growing radius). For tier 2, this becomes a neutron star instead of a regular star, dealing more damage and having a larger explosion..
    4. Small Torment Spheres - Fold mode attack. Fires a bunch of red spheres around its nearest target in a shotgun-like move. These all freeze midair for a moment, then fly inwards together straight at the target, exploding on contact for devastating damage). Reminiscent of the “Hellzone grenade” attack from Dragon Ball Z.
    5. Torment Beam - When below half health, it can use a beam attack that slowly points towards its nearest target. Alternating between following (pale red) and being stationary (red). Hard to avoid as it moves fast. While the target is inside the beam, they will be melted in the same way as the solar sphere attack can do. For tier 2, this beam moves faster.
  • 6. Tormentor Shield - Tier 2 only. The tormentor can fire out shield entities straight at you that block attacks and deal damage+knockback on collision.
  • 7. Time Distortion Wave - Tier 2 only. The tormentor spawns this attack directly on your position, a beautiful sphere of complex spiralling shapes. However, it is deadly, and if you don't leave it fast, you'll be frozen in place more and more until you can't move, leaving you vulnerable to other tormentor attacks. This mechanic comes from the same time freeze effect as the Chrono Cannon weapon can provide.
  • Ability to summon a wide array of powerful minions (any time you kill one of the first three summons, it deals 10 damage to the main Tormentor):
    1. Tormented Spider Moth (Chance to spawn when Tormentor is attacked. Stronger version of the Spider Moth boss that swoops at its target repeatedly, dealing heavy damage) - Blue glowing sphere hitbox, freezes in place for 2 seconds when attacked.
    2. Tormented Scorpioid Bloodluster (Chance to spawn when Tormentor is attacked. Stronger version of the Scorpioid Bloodluster boss that attacks you, can enter a forcefield mode where it fires projectiles, and can enter a lifesteal mode if it has just hit you with melee, where it flies over to the Tormentor and gives it health - hitting it stops it from doing so) - Orange glowing sphere hitbox, freezes in place for 2 seconds when attacked.
    3. Tormented Draconic Voidlasher (Chance to spawn when Tormentor is attacked. Stronger version of the Draconic Voidlasher boss that strafes around near to you, alternating between two modes - In mode 1, it shoots constant armour-piercing projectiles at you, and in mode 2, it it spawns homing spheres that fly at you after a few moments and deal significant damage on hit) - Purple glowing sphere hitbox, freezes in place for 2 seconds when attacked.
    4. Tormentor Larvae mobs. These mobs also spawn exclusively from the tormentor when in its "fold" attack phase, with varied size/strength ranges. They spawn near to the player and attack with long melee reach. Their hitbox is in their maws on the top.
    5. Tormentor Caterpillar mobs (and a giant invincible variant that is entirely particle-based) - these red caterpillars spawn during the splay attack phase, spiralling around the Tormentor and attacking any nearby tormentor targets by diving into them.
    6. Tormentor Tendril entity (Spawns occasionally when you get close to the maw. Killable. Has a curved particle line leading to the maw - slowly flies towards you, and if it gets close, it grabs you and attempts to pull you in. If it manages to pull you in, it will likely mean instant death.
  • Its attacks get stronger as it gets lower on health. Heavy torment blasts become faster, monstrous sphere attack begins, tormentor larvae, caterpillar, and tendril spawns increase, and small tormentor spheres become more numerous.
  • Tormenting nearby players (described in mechanics section above).

Other details

Other notes about the TORMENTOR:

  • Its scale is truly immense - approximately 680 blocks tall and 880 blocks in length.
  • Its health and position/rotation are controlled with global variables, rather than acting as an individual entity, meaning it can despawn and respawn with the health and attributes it left off at. However, it constantly force chunkloads its position to avoid needing to do this.
  • Switches slowly between three attack modes. Default (legs dangle), splay (legs splay out), and fold (back legs fold over front ones). Different attacks correspond to each mode.
  • Upon death, the tormentor clears weather in the current dimension
  • If you say the word "tormentor" in chat, it has a chance to angrily respond to you (with a cooldown after each attempt)
  • The TORMENTOR can be found in the void at the bottom of the crawling dimension (this is just a model and cannot attack or summon other arthropods).

Lore concept:

The Tormentor is an overwhelmingly powerful being that has accumulated at the bottom of the crawling from biological matter falling down the layers, evolving ever more harshly at each stage to eventually merge down into a singular ultimate lifeform that represents all of the horrors of the crawling (e.g. brutal evolution, demonic power, arthropod creepiness) combined and amplified by a scale equivalent to its vast size. An omnipotent, godlike entity using torment to its advantage - extreme suffering-induction being its main form of offense, making it virtually impossible for living beings to fight against. Moreover, it does not possess any interest in morality, and would casually subject everything to unlimited torment if this benefitted itself. Like the other bosses, it possesses supernatural powers, but amplifies them far beyond what the others are capable of instead of relying on physical combat. Such is its power that it can manifest in any location of its choosing and attack with a wide range of attacks and powerful summons without needing to move physically. The versions you encounter are mere avatars of its true limitless form, yet still possess unimaginable power - if its avatars are fought in The Crawling Dimension, they become invincible and cannot be destroyed. Its larvae may be candidates for a potential rebirth cycle where it continues to extend its reach beyond the crawling dimension. Despite the value of preserving nature, this is one lifecycle that unquestionably requires extinction!
